## ðŸ”‘ What Makes This Course DIFFERENT (Your USPs)

### 1. Rating-Based Progression (Not Topic-Based)
Most courses: "Week 1: Arrays, Week 2: Strings..." â€” boring, doesn't work for mixed levels.
**Your course:** Students enter at their rating level and progress through rating bands. A 1200-rated student and a 1500-rated student learn DP differently.

### 2. Dual-Track Assessment
Every 2 weeks, students take TWO assessments:
- **Placement Test:** LeetCode-style, 90 min, 3-4 problems (tests interview readiness)
- **CP Contest:** Codeforces-style, 2 hours, 5-6 problems (tests competitive ability)

### 3. The "Upsolving Culture"
After every contest:
- **Mandatory upsolving** of 1-2 unsolved problems within 48 hours
- **Editorial writing** â€” students write solutions for problems they solved (teaches deeply)
- **Peer review** â€” students review each other's code

### 4. ICPC Team Lab (Track 3 Exclusive)
- Form teams of 3
- Weekly 5-hour team contests
- Rotating roles: coder, thinker, navigator
- Video review of team performance

### 5. Placement Guarantee Layer
For Tracks 1 & 2:
- Resume building with competitive programming achievements
- Mock interviews every month
- Company-specific problem sets (Google, Amazon, Flipkart, etc.)
- OA (Online Assessment) simulation with time pressure
